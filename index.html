Light Grid — Build Views

Overhead mapping (interactive), side section, and rigging cut list in one place. 2,000 hung LED points · 10 control groups.

01Overhead plan & mapping viewed from the floor

10 groups: 2 for JESUS · 3 star groups · 5 arcs. JESUS splits into two groups along an arc boundary — hide it by matching each half to its arc, reveal it by lighting both a shared color. This is the audience's view — looking up at the underside from the floor — so the word reads correctly here; the controller pixel-map (defined from above) is its left↔right mirror, applied when the map is cut.

Reading the demo: waves roll upstage→downstage across the 5 arcs, the 3 star groups twinkle out of phase, then JESUS reveals — watch it lift out of the arcs and settle back in. Use JESUS hidden / shown to compare directly, and Group map for the raw assignment. Footprint 73′6″ × 58′6″, 18″ on center.

The JESUS camouflage: the two JESUS groups are split along an arc boundary, so when "hidden" each half wears the color of the arc crossing it and the word disappears. It's cleanest when the word crosses exactly two arcs — the camouflage % below the canvas shows how well the current curvature achieves that. (The default curvature of 53 is chosen so every group fits its controller zone's LED stock — check the zone legend in the hang guide before changing it.) The 3 star groups let the stars shimmer independently instead of blooming as one.

03Rigging & fishing-line cut list

One aircraft cable per row runs level across the width at the deck; each LED drops on its own fishing line. Lengths update with the sliders above.

Ordering allowance: add terminations to the cable (~1–2 ft per end) and knot/tie-off to every drop (~10–15%). Row 1 = downstage (shortest line); row 40 = upstage (longest). Each length is used ×50 — one per column across the width.

06Row-by-row hang guide volunteer build sequence · follows the sliders live

One strip per row cable, Row 1 (downstage, over the crowd) through Row 40 (upstage, over the stage). Positions run 1–50 left to right, exactly as drawn in the overhead plan above. Each numbered square is one LED — the number is the controller zone (= the stock bin) to pull from. The line under each strip reads the same sequence as counts: 6×Z2 means “six lights from Zone 2 in a row.” Line lengths follow the side-section trim sliders and match the cut list. This guide tracks the explorer live — the stamp below records the exact settings it was generated from. Reload the page to return to the defaults, and only hand this to volunteers once the design is locked.

Zone stock limits: Zones 1–2 hold 100 · Zones 3–4 hold 400 · Zones 5–6 hold 300 · Zones 7–8 hold 210 · Zones 9–10 hold 150. The legend reads count/stock — teal = fits with 10%+ spares, gold = fits with under 10% spares, red = over stock.

Orientation check before hanging: this guide (like the overhead plan) is the audience’s view from the floor, looking up. If you lay cables out on the deck or work from above, left↔right is mirrored — agree on which physical end of each cable is position 1 before attaching the first light.
